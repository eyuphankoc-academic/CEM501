### Item 1: Curtain Wall Schedule Delay (Schedule)

**Background:** The curtain wall system is **6 weeks behind** the original baseline schedule. The manufacturer (Fassaden GmbH, based in Stuttgart, Germany) has cited supply chain disruptions for the custom aluminum profiles on the south and east elevations.

The GC submitted a revised CPM schedule on April 1 showing a **4-week overall project delay** to the substantial completion date if no mitigation measures are taken. The curtain wall is on the critical path — it must be installed before the building envelope is sealed, which gates all interior finishes.

**Key facts on the table:**
- Original curtain wall delivery date: March 1, 2026
- Current manufacturer-quoted delivery date: April 12, 2026 (per last update from GC)
- Curtain wall installation duration: 10 weeks
- Building envelope seal required by: June 15, 2026 (to maintain finish schedule)
- Substantial completion date: September 1, 2026

**Discussion points:**
1. What is the current delivery status? Has the GC received an updated shipping confirmation from the manufacturer?
2. Who bears contractual responsibility for the delay — is this a force majeure event, a procurement failure, or a design-related delay?
3. What acceleration options are available? (extended work hours, additional installation crews, phased installation by elevation)
4. What is the estimated cost of acceleration, and how should that cost be allocated among the parties?
5. Does the revised schedule need to be formally accepted, or does the owner dispute the GC's critical path analysis?

### Item 3: Change Order #07 — Elevator Pit Rock Excavation (Cost)

**Background:** During excavation of the elevator pit (Grid Lines E-F / 3-4), the GC encountered **unexpected rock at elevation -4.50m**, approximately 1.2m above the design pit bottom at -5.70m. The original geotechnical report (Borings B-3 and B-7) did not identify rock at this location.

The GC submitted **Change Order #07 on March 15, 2026** for **1,200,000 TL**, covering:

| Item | Amount (TL) |
|------|-------------|
| Rock excavation (hydraulic breaker, 6 days) | 480,000 |
| Hauling and disposal of excavated rock (estimated 340 m³) | 185,000 |
| Additional formwork and concrete for irregular pit bottom | 120,000 |
| Waterproofing membrane modification | 65,000 |
| Equipment standby and crew redeployment during rock removal | 145,000 |
| General conditions, overhead, and profit (15%) | 205,000 |
| **Total** | **1,200,000** |

The Owner's Representative rejected the amount on March 28, citing concerns that the unit rates for rock excavation and the overhead markup exceed market rates for comparable work in Istanbul.

The Architect has not yet provided a cost evaluation or recommendation.

**Supporting documentation submitted by GC:**
- Daily field reports for the 6-day excavation period
- Equipment utilization logs (hydraulic breaker, excavator, haul trucks)
- Photographic documentation (before, during, and after)
- Letter from geotechnical subconsultant confirming rock was not anticipated by original borings
- Subcontractor invoices (rock excavation specialty sub)

**Discussion points:**
1. Is the scope of additional work legitimate? Does anyone dispute that the rock was unforeseen?
2. Has the architect reviewed the cost breakdown? What is the architect's recommendation?
3. Are the unit rates and quantities reasonable? Should an independent cost review be conducted?
4. What is the owner's position — full rejection, partial approval, or request for negotiation?
5. What is the timeline for resolution? The GC has indicated that unresolved change orders over 60 days may be escalated per the dispute resolution clause (General Conditions Article 12.1).

### Item 5: Laboratory Ventilation Redesign — Fume Hood Addition (Design Change)

**Background:** The university's Chemistry Department (Professor Erdogan) has requested the addition of **two fume hoods** to the 3rd-floor research laboratory (Room 312), which were not included in the original program. The existing ventilation design does not have sufficient capacity to serve the additional fume hoods.

**Preliminary assessment of required modifications:**

| Scope Element | Description |
|---------------|-------------|
| Ductwork | Larger main exhaust duct (from 400mm to 600mm diameter) serving Room 312 |
| Exhaust fan | Dedicated fume hood exhaust fan on roof (existing AHU cannot accommodate additional load) |
| Air handling | Potential upgrade of AHU-3 or addition of supplemental make-up air unit to maintain room pressure balance |
| Roof penetrations | 2-3 additional penetrations through the roof slab for ductwork and exhaust stack |
| Structural | Roof framing may require reinforcement to support larger/heavier mechanical equipment |
| Controls | Integration of fume hood face velocity sensors with the building management system |
| Electrical | Additional power circuit for exhaust fan motor (estimated 7.5 kW) |

No formal **Change Directive** or **Contemplated Change Order** has been issued by the owner. The request is currently verbal only.

**Timeline constraint:** The roofing subcontractor is scheduled to begin the roof membrane installation in approximately **6 weeks**. Any roof penetrations or structural modifications to the roof framing must be completed before this work begins. If the ventilation redesign is not finalized and approved within the next 3-4 weeks, the roof work will either need to be delayed or the penetrations will need to be cut after the fact — at significantly greater cost and with waterproofing risk.

**Discussion points:**
1. Is this a **scope change** (owner-directed addition) or **design development** (something the architect should have included)? This determines who pays for the design services.
2. Who initiates the formal Change Directive? What information does the owner need before issuing one?
3. Can the architect provide a preliminary design timeline? What information does the architect need from the structural engineer before proceeding?
4. Can the structural engineer confirm whether the existing roof framing can support the additional equipment, or is a detailed analysis required?
5. What is the estimated cost range for the full scope of work? Can the GC and MEP sub provide preliminary budget estimates?
6. What is the schedule impact? Is it feasible to complete this work before the roofing subcontractor mobilizes?
7. What is the decision timeline — by when must the owner issue a Change Directive to avoid delaying the roof work?
