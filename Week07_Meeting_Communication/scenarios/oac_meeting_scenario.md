## Meeting Agenda

### Item 1: Curtain Wall Schedule Delay (Schedule)

The curtain wall system is 6 weeks behind the original schedule. The manufacturer (based in Germany) has cited supply chain issues for the aluminum profiles. The GC submitted a revised schedule showing a 4-week overall project delay if no mitigation is taken.

**Discussion points:** Who bears responsibility? Can the schedule be recovered through acceleration? What are the cost implications?

### Item 2: RFI Backlog (Design Coordination)

There are currently 23 open RFIs, 14 of which have been outstanding for more than 21 days. The GC claims that work in several areas cannot proceed without design clarification, particularly around the laboratory exhaust systems and the 4th-floor structural connection details.

**Discussion points:** What is causing the delay in responses? Which RFIs are truly blocking work? Can any be resolved during this meeting?

### Item 3: Change Order #07 Dispute (Cost)

The GC has submitted Change Order #07 for 1.2 million TL, covering additional foundation work due to unexpected rock encountered during excavation of the elevator pit. The Owner's Rep has rejected the amount as excessive. The Architect has not yet provided a recommendation.

**Discussion points:** Is the scope of additional work legitimate? Is the pricing fair? What documentation supports the claim?

### Item 4: 4th-Floor Concrete Pour Coordination (Field Operations)

The 4th-floor slab pour is scheduled for next week. The structural engineer needs to confirm the revised rebar layout for the cantilever section. The MEP sub needs to confirm all embedded conduits and sleeves are placed. The pour requires a Saturday closure of the adjacent campus road.

**Discussion points:** Are all prerequisites met? Who is responsible for the road closure permit? What is the contingency if weather delays the pour?

### Item 5: Laboratory Ventilation Redesign (Design Change)

The university has requested an upgrade to the 3rd-floor laboratory ventilation to accommodate a fume hood addition (not in the original program). This requires larger ductwork, additional roof penetrations, and potentially a larger air handling unit. No formal change directive has been issued yet.

**Discussion points:** Is this a scope change or a design development issue? Who initiates the change directive? What is the schedule and cost impact?

## Facilitation Notes

**Duration:** 30-40 minutes of role-play + 15-20 minutes of debrief
**Format:** Seated around a table, Owner's Rep chairs the meeting
**Deliverable:** Each student submits a one-page reflection on what communication strategies worked and what they would change

### Debrief Questions
1. How did the hidden information affect the negotiation dynamics?
2. Which agenda items were resolved? Which were deferred? Why?
3. What communication behaviors helped move the meeting forward?
4. What behaviors created unnecessary conflict or stalled progress?
5. How would you run this meeting differently as the chair?
