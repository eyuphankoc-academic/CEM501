# CEM501: Communication Skills for CEM
## Week 13: Project Progress Presentations
**Spring 2026 | Dr. Eyuphan Koc | Bogazici University**

---

### Purpose

Week 13 is a checkpoint, not a final demo. Each student gives a **10-minute progress presentation** on their agent project. The goal is to surface technical and communication issues *before* Week 14, when the polished demo is graded.

Think of this as the "60% submittal review" before the final deliverable: enough to evaluate direction, early enough to course-correct.

---

### Format

| Block | Time |
|---|---|
| Presentation | 7 min |
| Q&A and feedback | 3 min |
| **Total per student** | **10 min** |

Order will be posted by end of Week 12 (randomized). All students attend all sessions — peer feedback is part of the exercise.

---

### What to Cover (7 minutes)

| Minute | Content |
|---|---|
| 0–1 | **Problem & scope.** What does your agent do, who is it for, what is the headline scenario? |
| 1–3 | **Architecture so far.** One diagram. Components, data flow, channels implemented. |
| 3–5 | **Live demo of one working scenario.** Even if rough. Show real input → real output. |
| 5–6 | **What is broken or missing.** Be honest. This is the most useful slide for feedback. |
| 6–7 | **Plan for Week 14.** What you will fix, add, or polish before the final demo. |

---

### Q&A (3 minutes)

Expect questions on:

- Design decisions you cannot yet justify
- Edge cases your demo did not show
- How your remaining two weeks of work will close the gap to the M9 rubric

Practice the same techniques from Week 14 prep: paraphrase the question, answer honestly, say "I don't know yet, but here is how I would investigate it" when appropriate.

---

### Submission

Push the current state of your repository to GitHub **before your presentation slot**. The instructor and your peers should be able to see:

- Working code (even if incomplete)
- A draft `ARCHITECTURE.md`
- A populated `agent.log` from at least one test run

No formal write-up is required for Week 13. The presentation itself is the deliverable.

---

### Evaluation

Week 13 is **ungraded but mandatory**. Missing your slot without prior arrangement costs **5 points from your final M9 grade**. The point of this week is feedback — not assessment.

You will receive:

- Verbal feedback from the instructor during your Q&A
- Written peer feedback (3–5 sentences) from at least 5 classmates, delivered via the course portal within 48 hours

---

### Setup Checklist

Complete the night before your slot:

- [ ] Agent runs end-to-end for at least one scenario
- [ ] One architecture diagram ready (slide, image, or whiteboard sketch)
- [ ] Repository pushed to GitHub
- [ ] Laptop charged; HDMI/USB-C adapter packed
- [ ] Timed your presentation at least once — under 7 minutes

---

> **Key Insight:** A construction submittal review is not about catching the architect out — it is about finding the problems while they are still cheap to fix. Treat your Week 13 presentation the same way. The goal is not to look polished. The goal is to leave with a clearer plan for Week 14 than you arrived with.
